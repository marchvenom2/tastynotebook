Chicken Biryani Complete Guide: Layering, Rice Texture, Spice Balance & Dum Cooking

Updated: June 26, 2026

1) Ingredient Planning and Mise en Place

Great biryani starts with prep. Measure spices, slice onions, marinate chicken, and soak basmati rice before heat is turned on...

2) Marination for Flavor Depth

Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, lemon juice, and salt. Resting time improves tenderness and helps spices penetrate...

3) Rice Parboiling for Correct Grain Texture

Cook basmati in heavily salted water with whole spices until 70–80% done. Grain should break with a firm core, not fully soft...

4) Build the Masala Base

Brown onions properly, cook tomatoes until moisture reduces, and bloom spices carefully to avoid bitterness. The masala should be rich but not watery...

5) Layering for Aroma and Structure

Alternate cooked chicken masala and rice layers. Add fried onions, herbs, saffron milk, and ghee for aroma and visual appeal...

6) Dum Cooking Technique

Seal pot and cook on low heat so steam finishes rice and integrates flavors. Use a heavy-bottom pan or heat diffuser to prevent burning...

7) Resting, Fluffing, and Serving

Rest the biryani before opening. Fluff gently from sides to avoid breaking grains. Serve with raita, salad, and lemon wedges...

8) Storage and Reheating

Cool leftovers safely, refrigerate promptly, and reheat with a splash of water over low heat to retain moisture...

Conclusion

Perfect biryani is about control: marination, rice doneness, masala moisture, and gentle dum heat. Master these and results become repeatable.
